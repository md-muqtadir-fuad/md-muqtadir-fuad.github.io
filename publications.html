eGov-Lens: A Multi-Dimensional Machine Learning Approach to Aspect-Based Public Feedback Analysis on Bengali e-Government Platforms

Conference: 2025 28th International Conference on Computer and Information Technology (ICCIT)

Md. Muqtadir Fuad, Abdullah Al Mazid, Md. Istiak Adnan, Tirthojit Das, Shatabdi Sen Prapti & Nafisa Mahbub

View Paper → PDF

A comprehensive study on aspect-based sentiment analysis for low-resource Bengali language, utilizing a fine-tuned mBERT architecture to process public sentiment on government services.

Abstract: With the rapid expansion of e-services under Digital Bangladesh initiatives, citizens increasingly rely on online platforms to access government services and most often share their opinion in social media and digital forums. Existing sentiment analysis lacks of aspect-level insights in terms of egovernment related services. This paper presents eGov-Lens, a practical pipeline for aspect-based analysis of Bengali public feedback on e-government channels. We curate and annotate 34,001 comments (2020-2024) spanning twelve services and four policy-relevant aspects- sentiment, ease of find, responsiveness, and accuracy. The approach benchmarks three text representations (bag-of-words/CountVectorizer, BanglaBERT, mBERT) with logistic regression, support vector classifier, and naïve bayes. BanglaBERT with a support-vector head attains the strongest single-aspect quality on the policy-critical Accuracy task (micro-avg accuracy ≈0.93), while BanglaBERT with logistic regression offers the best accuracy-latency trade-off for deployment and a classical CountVectorizer+LR baseline trains and infers fastest. An entropy-weighted TOPSIS combines quality and cost to rank alternatives, favoring CountVectorizer+LR overall with BanglaBERT+LR a close second, and providing actionable guidance for NLP deployment on resource-constrained government infrastructure.

Stochastic Modeling of Throughput-Quality Dynamics in Labor-Intensive Manufacturing: A Comparative Analysis of Operator Fatigue and Process Instability

Conference:8th IEOM Bangladesh International Conference on Industrial Engineering and Operations Management

Md. Muqtadir Fuad, Abdullah Al Mazid, Md. Istiak Adnan & Khalid Ammar

View Paper → PDF

Presents a stochastic and comparative analysis of operator fatigue and process instability in labor-intensive manufacturing using time-motion study, statistical process control, regression analysis, process capability analysis, and Monte Carlo simulation.

Abstract: The inherent variability of manual assembly processes presents significant challenges to standardization and quality assurance within the semi-automated food production sector. This study presents a quantitative and comparative analysis of the stochastic nature of human fatigue and its effect on productivity. By integrating high-resolution time-motion analysis with statistical process control (SPC), the research investigates two manual production lines to quantify quality parameters and examine relationships among product shape, operator performance behavior, efficiency, and unit weight consistency. Regression analysis was employed to estimate the relationship between time and worker fatigue in a controlled environment. Furthermore, process capability analysis reveals the relationship between human fatigue and process instability. Finally, using a Monte Carlo algorithm, the study simulates data to quantify annual financial loss due to process instability.

IoT-Based Smart Conveyor System for Integrated Quality, Inventory and Process Control

Technical Poster

Md. Muqtadir Fuad, Abdullah Al Mazid, Md. Ahabab Ul Haq, Most. Noshin Ibnat, Chowdhury Mouinur Rahman & Nafis Ahmad

View Poster → PDF

Details the architecture of a cost-effective, real-time quality control conveyor prototype built with Arduino and ESP32 to demonstrate Industry 4.0 concepts in typical manufacturing workflows.

Abstract:This project presents an IoT-based smart conveyor system designed for automated weight-triggered operation and real-time inventory tracking. Using a 5kg load cell with an HX711 amplifier, the system detects when an item’s weight exceeds a preset threshold (e.g., 200 grams) to start the conveyor motor controlled by an L298N driver and a 6V 600RPM DC gear motor. A mechanical arm, operated by an SG-90 micro servo motor, places items onto the conveyor belt. The HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor detects and counts each passing item, providing immediate visual feedback through a green LED. The ESP32 microcontroller enables IoT connectivity, allowing remote monitoring and real-time data logging of weight, item count, and system status for efficient inventory management and process control. This automated setup enhances accuracy, efficiency, and quality assurance in manufacturing lines.

Aerodynamic Performance Analysis of NACA 4415 Airfoil Incorporating Gurney and Bionic Flap: A Steady-State Numerical Analysis

Conference: 7th Bangladesh Conference on Industrial Engineering and Operations Management

Md Zisanul Haque Bhuiyan, Md. Muqtadir Fuad, Masud Rana, A. S. M. Maruf Hasan & Khalid Ammar

View Paper → PDF

Presents a steady-state CFD-based comparative analysis of standard NACA 4415 airfoil, NACA 4415 with Gurney flap, and NACA 4415 with bionic flap using lift coefficient, drag coefficient, lift-to-drag ratio, and pressure-velocity contours.

Abstract: NACA 4415 is one of the most common and widely studied airfoils in aerodynamic research. Incorporating flaps with the NACA 4415 airfoil is an effective way to improve aerodynamic performance and efficiency. This study presents a comparative numerical analysis among a standard NACA 4415 airfoil, a modified NACA 4415 airfoil incorporating a Gurney flap, and a NACA 4415 airfoil incorporating a bionic flap based on the coefficient of lift and coefficient of drag at various angles of attack, including -15°, -10°, -5°, 0°, 5°, 10°, and 15°, with a flap height of 2% of the chord length. The airfoil performance was evaluated under a constant upstream velocity of 20 m/s using Computational Fluid Dynamics simulations. Performance was measured based on the lift-to-drag coefficient ratio and validated using velocity and pressure contours over the surface of the airfoil. The highest lift-to-drag ratio was found in the NACA 4415 airfoil incorporating a Gurney flap, followed by the standard NACA 4415 airfoil, while the NACA 4415 airfoil incorporating a bionic flap showed the lowest ratio. At 0° angle of attack, the Gurney flap improved the lift-to-drag ratio by 49.9% compared to the standard airfoil, while the bionic flap reduced the ratio by 19.86%. Overall, the NACA 4415 airfoil with Gurney flap outperformed the other two airfoil configurations, and the velocity and pressure contours supported the results obtained from lift and drag coefficients.
